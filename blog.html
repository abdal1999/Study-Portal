🌱 May 2025 – Inside the Mind of a Class 10 Student in 2025: More Than Just Exams

Published: May 27, 2025

In 2025, being a Class 10 student means carrying a lot more than just a bag full of books. It’s a phase packed with pressure, decisions, dreams, and a silent battle to stay afloat. For many, it’s the first real taste of what it feels like to be “under the scanner” where marks, performance, and expectations start to shape how others see you, and sometimes how you see yourself.

Read Full Blog →

The pressure hasn’t gone anywhere. The phrase “board exams” still makes hearts race , it still means sleepless nights, high-stakes tests, and a sense that everything depends on how well you perform in a handful of papers. Parents want the best for their kids, teachers push for results, relatives ask questions, and sometimes even strangers have something to say. But very few pause to ask students what they’re going through. The pressure is real, and often, it’s invisible to those not living it every day.

What makes it even harder is the never-ending routine. There’s barely a moment to breathe between revisions, class tests, assignments, and mock exams. Every week feels like another countdown. There’s a constant feeling that time is running out, the syllabus is too long, and that “perfect score” everyone talks about is just out of reach. Yet, in the middle of all this, students keep showing up. They adapt. They figure out how to manage time, how to stay focused, and how to push themselves , even when it’s hard.

But behind the scenes, there’s a whole other story. The mental load of being a student today is heavy. The comparisons, the self-doubt, the fear of not being “good enough” , it adds up. These things aren’t written in notebooks or visible in report cards, but they’re there. We talk a lot about academic performance, but not nearly enough about how students are feeling. It’s time we change that. Instead of just asking, “How did the test go?” maybe we should be asking, “How are you doing?”

Class 10 isn’t just about academics. It’s also a deeply emotional year. It’s when students start thinking seriously about what they want in life , what subjects to take, what stream to choose, what kind of future to imagine. And at the same time, it’s one of the last years to enjoy school life in its truest form, laughing with friends, celebrating birthdays in class, playing during recess. It’s a strange mix of growing up fast and trying to hold on to what’s still left of childhood.

What students often need most right now isn’t more advice it’s understanding. They need someone who won’t judge them for feeling overwhelmed, someone who’ll listen instead of lecture. They need space to figure things out, make mistakes, and learn at their own pace.

Yes, marks do matter. But they don’t tell the full story. What really counts is what students are becoming in the process more resilient, more responsible, and more self-aware. They're not just learning facts and formulas. They're learning how to deal with pressure, how to stay grounded, and how to keep going even when things get tough. They’re preparing for more than just exams. They’re preparing for life.

So, to every Class 10 student out there you may not hear it enough, but you’re doing a great job. It’s okay to feel overwhelmed. It’s okay to not have all the answers. Just keep going, one day at a time. You’re stronger than you think.

And to every adult reading this be the support system, not the scoreboard. What students need most right now isn’t judgment. It’s understanding.
